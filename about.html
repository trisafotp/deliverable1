“In life, there are two things which are dependable: the pleasures of the flesh and the pleasures of literature.”
-- Sei Shonagon, The Pillow Book.

I’m at a loss for what to say about myself. Below are several short lists of books and movies that I admire — that, in words of Sei Shonagon, “quicken [my] heart.” Perhaps they’ll bring you the pleasure they’ve brought me.

Fiction Worth Reading:

  • Life: A User’s Manual by Georges Perec
  • The Mezzanine by Nicholson Baker
  • Lanark: A Life in Four Books by Alasdair Gray

Movies Worth Watching:

  • Sans Soleil by Chris Marker
  • World of Tomorrow by Don Hertzfeldt
  • 2046 by Wong Kar Wai
